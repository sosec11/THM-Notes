# TryHackMe - Become a Defender

## Introduction

The "Become a Defender" module introduces the defensive side of cybersecurity.

Instead of attacking systems, defenders focus on **protecting systems, detecting threats, and responding to incidents**.

This module provides an overview of:

- defensive cybersecurity roles
- monitoring and detection
- incident response
- security operations

---

## What is a Defender?

A defender is a cybersecurity professional responsible for protecting systems from attacks.

Their objectives include:

- preventing attacks
- detecting suspicious activity
- responding to incidents
- minimizing damage

Defenders play a critical role in maintaining system security and resilience.

---

## Blue Team vs Red Team

Cybersecurity is often divided into two main roles:

- **Red Team** → attackers (offensive security)
- **Blue Team** → defenders (defensive security)

Defenders (Blue Team) focus on:

- monitoring systems
- analyzing logs
- detecting threats
- responding to incidents

---

## Common Defensive Roles

### SOC Analyst

Works in a **Security Operations Center (SOC)**.

Responsibilities:

- monitor alerts
- analyze suspicious activity
- respond to incidents

---

### Security Analyst

- investigates threats
- analyzes logs and events
- improves detection systems

---

### Incident Responder

- handles security incidents
- contains and mitigates attacks
- performs forensic analysis

---

### Security Engineer

- builds and maintains secure systems
- implements security controls
- configures monitoring tools

---

## Key Defensive Concepts

### Monitoring

Continuous observation of systems and networks to detect unusual behavior.

---

### Logging

Recording system and application activity.

Logs are essential for:

- detecting attacks
- investigating incidents
- understanding system behavior

---

### Alerting

Generating alerts when suspicious activity is detected.

---

### Incident Response

The process of handling security incidents, including:

- identification
- containment
- eradication
- recovery

---

## Security Tools

Defenders use various tools, such as:

- SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)
- IDS/IPS (Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems)
- antivirus and endpoint protection
- log analysis tools

---

## Why Defensive Security Matters

Organizations are constantly targeted by cyber threats.

Defensive security helps:

- detect attacks early
- reduce impact
- protect sensitive data
- maintain system availability

Without defenders, systems would be vulnerable to constant compromise.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Defenders protect systems against cyber threats.
- Blue Team focuses on detection and response.
- Monitoring, logging, and alerting are essential.
- Incident response is a critical process.
- Defensive roles are key in cybersecurity operations.

The **Become a Defender** module introduces the foundations of defensive cybersecurity.
