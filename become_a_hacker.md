# TryHackMe - Become a Hacker

## Introduction

The "Become a Hacker" module introduces the fundamentals of cybersecurity and what it means to work as an ethical hacker.

It provides an overview of:

- hacking concepts
- types of hackers
- career paths in cybersecurity
- learning platforms and tools

This module is designed to help beginners understand how to start in cybersecurity.

---

## What is Hacking?

Hacking refers to identifying and exploiting weaknesses in systems.

In cybersecurity, ethical hackers use their skills to:

- find vulnerabilities
- improve system security
- protect organizations

Hacking is not inherently illegal — it depends on intent and authorization.

---

## Types of Hackers

### White Hat Hackers

- ethical hackers
- work legally to improve security
- perform penetration testing

---

### Black Hat Hackers

- malicious hackers
- exploit systems for personal gain
- perform illegal activities

---

### Grey Hat Hackers

- operate between ethical and unethical boundaries
- may access systems without permission but without malicious intent

---

## Common Cybersecurity Roles

Cybersecurity offers various career paths:

- **Penetration Tester (Pentester)** → simulates attacks to find vulnerabilities
- **Security Analyst** → monitors systems and detects threats
- **SOC Analyst** → works in a Security Operations Center
- **Security Engineer** → builds secure systems

Each role requires different skills but shares common foundations.

---

## Key Skills for a Hacker

To become a hacker, you need to develop skills in:

- networking fundamentals
- operating systems (Linux & Windows)
- programming (Python, JavaScript, etc.)
- web technologies
- security concepts

Continuous learning and practice are essential.

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

Ethical hacking must always be:

- authorized
- legal
- responsible

Unauthorized hacking is illegal and can lead to serious consequences.

Always practice in controlled environments like labs or training platforms.
